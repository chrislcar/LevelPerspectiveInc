# P1.1 Step 9 — Cross-Element Integration

## Sync Facilitation Spec · Virtual Variant

This spec governs the 90-minute virtual integration session that compiles Slide 1 from the four locked element deliverables produced by Steps 4, 5, 6, and 8. It inherits the canonical six-move structure from Block D (Step 6 sync facilitation spec) and applies it to the integration job.

## 2. Session fields

### (a) What this step is

A 90-minute structured integration session in which the leadership team converts four locked element inputs (Strategic Direction from Step 4, Brand Image from Step 5, Organizational Culture from Step 6, Center from Step 8) plus the AI's cross-element synthesis into the composite Slide 1 deliverable: four populated elements at postcard altitude, three named H&S gap annotations between adjacent elements, attribution footer, and named dissent preserved.

The session conducts cross-element gap synthesis live (Vision–Image, Image–Culture, Culture–Vision per Hatch & Schultz), stress-tests the synthesized Center as coherent throughline against the three petals, screens the Center against the identity-vs-aspiration anti-pattern surfaced in V3, and locks Slide 1 in postcard altitude.

### (b) Objective

Three commitments, in order of altitude:

1. A composite Slide 1 deliverable: four committed elements at postcard altitude, three H&S gap annotations placed visually between elements, attribution footer present, named dissent preserved.
2. A diagnosed Center that anchors all three petals as articulable throughline (or carries explicit yellow/red flags where it does not), with aspirational drift relocated to P1.2 handoff.
3. A structured handoff doc carrying gap evidence, contested items, aspirational-Center carry-forwards, and parking-lot content into P1.2 Decision 1 substrate.

What this session deliberately does NOT do:

- Rework any of the four locked element contents (that is Step 4/5/6/8 territory; reopening it breaks the U-shape).
- Resolve any of the three H&S gaps (resolution is future-state work in P1.2/P1.5; Step 9 maps gaps, does not close them).
- Commit to any future-state Center, Direction, Image, or Culture (P1.2 work).
- Make Decision 1, 2, or 3 (all P1.2).

### (c) How this step achieves its objective

A six-move sequence with named techniques per move, AI cross-element synthesis as priming input, sticky color discipline throughout, dissent capture in real time. The session compresses three substantively distinct integration tasks (gap synthesis, Center stress-test, Slide 1 compile) into 80–85 working minutes plus 5–10 minutes structural overhead.

The compression depends on three preconditions:

1. The four element deliverables arrive locked from Steps 4, 5, 6, 8 — Step 9 does not relitigate their content.
2. The AI synthesis has proposed candidate gaps and flagged contradictions 24 h pre-session — the room interrogates candidates rather than generates from blank.
3. The room arrives framework-literate (V1–V4 and V_oc all watched).

If any of the three preconditions fails, the session does not run.

### (d) Deliverable produced

| Artifact | Format | Owner |
|---|---|---|
| Slide 1 composite — four elements + three gap annotations + attribution footer | Slide 1 frame on FigJam (later exported to deck) | Methodologist (custodian); CEO commits |
| Center anchoring assessment — three arrows, color-coded (green / yellow / red) | Center-anchor arrow frame on FigJam | Methodologist |
| Three H&S gap statements with one-line evidence pointers | Gap-annotation gutters between elements | Methodologist (drafts placement); room drafts content |
| Aspirational-Center carry-forward list (input to P1.2 Decision 1) | Parking-lot frame on FigJam + structured handoff doc | Methodologist |
| Dissent log | FigJam dissent panel + structured handoff doc | Producer (real-time capture) |
| P1.2 handoff doc — Slide 1 + dissent + parking-lot + gap evidence + Center carry-forward ratio | Structured doc per P4.5 template | Methodologist (compiles); producer (formats) |

### (e) Coaching role

Methodologist surfaces evidence and structures the integration, never authors interpretations.

The methodologist does NOT:

- Propose gap content (the room generates gap statements via 1-2-4-All).
- Propose what the Center→petal throughline is (the room speaks it; methodologist holds the pen).
- Pick between aspirational and identity Center items (the room screens them).
- Draft postcard-altitude language (the room drafts; methodologist enforces the 8-word ceiling).

The methodologist DOES:

- Enforce sticky color discipline.
- Hold the time budget.
- Surface AI-flagged contradictions for the room to interrogate.
- Prompt for evidence specificity ("which element card supports that gap claim?").
- Call out when the room drifts into resolution rather than mapping.
- Capture dissent verbatim.
- Prevent element-content rework from sneaking back into a session that exists to integrate locked inputs.

## 5. Session structure — six moves in 90 minutes

| Move | Duration | Sub-step | Technique | Output |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | 5 min | Open | Frame + readiness check + AI synthesis surface | Time budget agreed; pre-work confirmed; AI candidates surfaced |
| 1 | 10 min | Validate four element inputs (9.1) | ORID I-phase across all four elements | Validated inputs with carried-forward dissent flagged |
| 2 | 25 min | Cross-element gap synthesis (9.2) | 1-2-4-All across V–I, I–C, C–V | 3 gap statements with evidence pointers |
| 3 | 18 min | Center anchoring test (9.3) | Live drawing on FigJam; methodologist holds the pen | 3 Center→petal arrows, color-coded |
| 4 | 8 min | Identity-vs-aspiration screen (9.4) | Rapid round-the-room with 70% threshold protocol | Aspirational-Center items relocated; ship/halt determined |
| 5 | 18 min | Slide 1 compile (9.5) | Min Specs + Consensus Workshop resolve | Composite Slide 1 with attribution and gap annotations |
| 6 | 6 min | Close | Confirm commitments + dissent + handoff | Deliverable completion |

### Move 0 — Open (5 min)

**Method:**

1. (1 min) Methodologist re-states what this session does (integrate four locked elements into Slide 1; map three H&S gaps; stress-test Center) and does not (rework element content; resolve gaps; commit future-state).
2. (1 min) Producer confirms five videos watched, four elements locked, AI synthesis read by all 7 ELT members (round-the-room thumbs).
3. (2 min) Methodologist surfaces the AI synthesis's three highest-confidence findings and three flagged cross-element contradictions — *for the room to interrogate, not endorse*.
4. (1 min) Sticky color and postcard-altitude reminder.

**Methodologist neutrality test:** does not endorse or deny the AI's findings; surfaces them for the room.

### Move 1 — Validate four element inputs (10 min, sub-step 9.1)

**Method (ORID I-phase):**

1. (4 min) Methodologist walks the canvas: each of the four locked elements displayed in turn (Strategic Direction, Brand Image, Organizational Culture, Center). Names the carried-forward dissent flagged red on each from Steps 4, 5, 6, 8.
2. (4 min) **One question only**: *"Looking at all four elements together, what's missing or wrong before we can integrate?"* Round-the-room, max 30 seconds each.
3. (2 min) Methodologist captures issues on red (contested) stickies in the relevant element frame. **Element content does not get rewritten in Step 9** — issues raised here are flagged forward to Slide 1 as red items, not back-routed to Step 4/5/6/8.

**Output:** Four validated element inputs with named contestations preserved as red flags for Slide 1 carry-through.

**Methodologist neutrality test:** does not adjudicate contestations; does not allow re-opening of locked element content.

### Move 2 — Cross-element gap synthesis (25 min, sub-step 9.2)

**Method (1-2-4-All across three H&S gaps):**

1. (4 min) Solo silent: each ELT member writes one short gap statement (max 12 words) for each of the three H&S gaps based on the four elements in front of them — *Vision–Image* (Strategic Direction ↔ Brand Image), *Image–Culture* (Brand Image ↔ Organizational Culture), *Culture–Vision* (Organizational Culture ↔ Strategic Direction). Reference the AI synthesis candidates as priming, not as answers.
2. (5 min) Pairs (3 pairs + 1 methodologist-paired solo): each pair commits one gap statement per gap.
3. (8 min) Quartets: pairs combine into two quartets. Each quartet converges on one gap statement per gap, attaching a one-line evidence pointer (which element card or AI-flagged contradiction supports it).
4. (8 min) All: methodologist runs round-table. Each quartet posts gap statements in the gutter between the relevant elements. Methodologist places quartet-disagreement items as alternative candidates without resolving — Move 5 resolves.

**Discipline:**

- Gap statements describe present-tense observed misalignment, not aspirational alignment ("stated AI-first direction; market perceives reliability-focus" — not "we should align direction with image").
- A gap with no evidence pointer is yellow (hypothesis), not green.
- The AI synthesis's candidate gap statements are priming, not authority. The room can adopt, reject, or rewrite.
- The room is mapping gaps, not resolving them. Resolution is P1.2 Decision 1 territory.

**Output:** Three gap statements (one per H&S gap), each with one-line evidence pointer; alternative candidates preserved if quartets disagreed.

**Methodologist neutrality test:** does not propose gap content; does not pick between quartet candidates; redirects any conversation drifting toward "what should we do about it" with *"that is P1.2 work — we are mapping, not resolving."*

### Move 3 — Center anchoring test (18 min, sub-step 9.3)

**Method (live drawing, methodologist holds the pen):**

1. (6 min) **Center → Strategic Direction.** Methodologist draws an arrow from Center to Strategic Direction on the canvas. Round-the-room one-liner per ELT member: *"What's the throughline that makes our Strategic Direction read as a natural expression of our Center?"* Methodologist colors the arrow green (room can articulate the throughline in 90 sec or less, with consensus), yellow (room can articulate but with named gaps), or red (room cannot articulate — Center does not anchor this petal).
2. (6 min) **Center → Organizational Culture.** Same procedure. Same coloring rule.
3. (6 min) **Center → Brand Image.** Same procedure. Same coloring rule. (Note: this arrow is the longest on the canvas, traversing the column gap to reach the bottom-full element. The reach itself is sometimes diagnostic — Brand Image is the most external of the three petals, and a yellow or red arrow here often signals overclaim or underclaim rather than anchor failure.)

**Discipline:**

- This is a **coherence test**, not a commitment test. The question is *"does the synthesized Center as articulated explain what we found in this petal?"* — not *"does Center commit us to anything going forward?"* Forward-anchor work is P1.2 / Decision 1.
- Yellow and red arrows are valid outcomes. They surface diagnostic substrate for P1.2.
- The room is not asked to fix a yellow or red arrow in Step 9. Center content does not get rewritten here; the arrow color carries forward.
- A red arrow on any single petal is sufficient to flag the Center quadrant red on Slide 1.

**Output:** Three Center→petal arrows, color-coded green/yellow/red. Any non-green arrow becomes part of the P1.2 handoff substrate.

**Methodologist neutrality test:** holds the pen but does not propose throughlines; does not soften a red call into yellow or a yellow call into green.

### Move 4 — Identity-vs-aspiration screen (8 min, sub-step 9.4)

**Method (rapid):**

1. (1 min) Methodologist re-displays Center quadrant content.
2. (5 min) Round-the-room one observation per person (max 45 sec each): *"For each item in Center: is this who we are right now, or who we want to be?"*
3. (2 min) Methodologist applies the threshold protocol below and either relocates aspirational items to the parking lot (if proceeding) or halts the session (if escalating).

**70% threshold protocol:**

- **If ≤70% of Step 8's Center items fail the screen:** relocate the aspirational subset to the parking lot ("Center carry-forward → P1.2 Decision 1") and proceed to Move 5 with the surviving identity content. Items contested in either direction (room disagrees on identity vs aspiration) get split: the identity portion stays in Center; the aspirational portion relocates; the disagreement is logged in the dissent panel.
- **If >70% of Step 8's Center items fail the screen:** methodologist halts Move 5, escalates to engagement sponsor (per P4.8), and reschedules Step 9 after a Step 8 rerun. The session does not proceed to compile.

The 70% line is a Step 8 quality trigger, not a Step 9 design switch — it does not bias the methodologist toward shipping or halting. The methodologist applies it mechanically.

**Discipline:**

- Aspiration is not banned — it is *relocated* (under the threshold) or it triggers a rerun (over the threshold). Aspirational content under threshold becomes P1.2 input, not Slide 1 content.
- This move surfaces drift; it does not interrogate Step 8's quality directly. Step 8 quality is interrogated only by the threshold trigger.
- The room is not asked to defend aspirational items as identity. Disagreement → split or relocate, not debate.

**Output (under threshold):** Center content with aspirational items relocated; ≥1 aspirational item in the parking lot for P1.2 handoff (zero is suspicious; a clean screen often signals the room collapsed the test rather than running it).

**Output (over threshold):** Session halted; engagement sponsor notified; reschedule authorization requested; no Slide 1 produced this session.

**Methodologist neutrality test:** does not propose which items are aspirational; does not adjudicate room disagreement on whether an item is identity or aspiration; applies the 70% threshold mechanically without pleading either way.

### Move 5 — Slide 1 compile (18 min, sub-step 9.5)

**Method (Min Specs + Consensus Workshop resolve):**

1. (4 min) **Min Specs framing.** Solo silent write: *"What is the absolute minimum that has to land on each element for this slide to be diagnostically defensible?"*
2. (6 min) **Round-the-room dot vote.** Each ELT member places dots on candidate items per element: 3 dots on Center (post Move-4 cleanup), 3 dots on Strategic Direction, 3 dots on Brand Image, 3 dots on Organizational Culture. Methodologist also surfaces the three gap statements from Move 2 for confirmation (not voting — gap statements either survive Move 5 confirmation or are flagged red).
3. (5 min) **Consensus Workshop resolve.** Methodologist asks per element: *"Can the room commit to these top-voted items as the current-state picture?"* If contested, methodologist captures dissent verbatim and proceeds with the contested item flagged red on Slide 1. Same resolve question applies to gap annotations.
4. (3 min) **Postcard altitude check.** Methodologist re-states each committed item in postcard-altitude language (max 8 words). Confirms attribution footer ("Strategyzer's adaptation of Hatch & Schultz's VCI model") is present. Confirms gap annotations are placed visually between the relevant elements.

**Discipline:**

- Slide 1 commitment is to the **current-state articulation as integrated**, not to its desirability or future commitment.
- This move does not re-open element content. Items raised in Move 1 as contested ride forward as red flags on Slide 1, not as element rewrites.
- Postcard altitude (max 8 words per item) is enforced symmetrically across all four elements.
- Dissent is preserved on the slide if contested. **Better a red-flagged Slide 1 than a false-consensus Slide 1.**
- A Center quadrant flagged red by Move 3 (any non-green Center→petal arrow) carries that flag onto Slide 1 even if Move 5 voting is uncontested.
- Because the canvas and the Slide 1 compile frame share geometry, this move is lock-and-postcard, not spatial translation.

**Output:** Slide 1 — composite four-element deliverable at postcard altitude in Brightworks-aligned SGF geometry, three gap annotations placed between relevant elements, attribution footer, contested items flagged red, aspirational items captured separately for P1.2 handoff.

**Methodologist neutrality test:** does not draft postcard-altitude language; the room drafts and the methodologist enforces the 8-word ceiling and asks for compression where needed. Does not pick between contested items.

### Move 6 — Close (6 min)

**Method:**

1. (2 min) Methodologist re-displays composite Slide 1.
2. (1 min) Methodologist re-states what was deferred to P1.2: aspirational Center items, contested items, gap annotations as input to Decision 1, any yellow/red Center→petal arrows, Center carry-forward ratio.
3. (1 min) Producer confirms dissent log captured; FigJam permissions set; structured handoff doc generated and circulating.
4. (1 min) Methodologist re-states next session (Step 11 BMC mapping if Slide 2 is being authored next; or Step 1 of P1.2 if all of Slides 1, 2, 3, and Appendix 16 are complete).
5. (1 min) Energy check: round-the-room one-word reaction.

## 6. Quality bar (60-second self-check before declaring Compile complete)

- ☐ Three H&S gap statements (V–I, I–C, C–V) each carry a one-line evidence pointer; yellow if hypothesis only.
- ☐ All three Center→petal anchor arrows are colored (green / yellow / red); no uncolored arrow leaves the canvas.
- ☐ Identity-vs-aspiration screen produced ≥1 relocated item OR the room defended Center as fully identity verbatim.
- ☐ Slide 1 items are postcard altitude — max 8 words each. Compress before committing.
- ☐ Attribution footer present: "Strategyzer's adaptation of Hatch & Schultz's VCI model."
- ☐ Contested items flagged red on Slide 1; dissent log carries verbatim into P1.2 handoff doc.
- ☐ Locked elements from Steps 4, 5, 6, 8 were not rewritten in Step 9 — issues rode forward as red flags.

Failure on any item: fix before declaring Slide 1 complete. Compile is not the same as ship — ship needs all checks green.

## 7. Risks and mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| One or more elements arrive weakly populated (Step 4/5/6/8 underperformance) | Medium-low | Run Move 1 to flag; carry weak-element red flags onto Slide 1 rather than reworking in 90 min |
| AI cross-element synthesis pre-read not done by ≥3 ELT members | Medium | Move 0 absorbs minor cost; if >2 ELT members unread, escalate to producer for reschedule rather than running underprepared |
| Room cannot articulate Center→petal throughline on any single arrow (yellow or red Move 3 outcome) | Medium | Preserve as evidence; Slide 1 ships with non-green Center arrow flagged; flag is P1.2 substrate not Step 9 rework trigger |
| Identity-vs-aspiration screen surfaces majority of Center as aspiration | Medium-low if Step 8 was disciplined; high if not | ≤70% fail → relocate to P1.2 handoff, ship Slide 1 with thinner Center flagged red. >70% fail → halt Move 5, escalate to engagement sponsor (P4.8), reschedule after Step 8 rerun |
| Gap synthesis surfaces a contradiction the room wants to fight about | Medium-high | Methodologist redirects: *"We are mapping the gap, not resolving it. P1.2 owns resolution."* Capture contested gap with both characterizations preserved |
| Room attempts to rework locked element content | Medium-high | Producer's standing job: catch any "let's just fix Strategic Direction quickly" drift and flag in real time. Methodologist redirects to red-flag-on-Slide-1 path |
| Postcard altitude breaks down (room insists on 14-word items) | Low-medium | Methodologist enforces compression; if room genuinely cannot compress, the underlying content is probably two items being conflated and gets split |
| Methodologist drifts into authoring (proposes throughline language; picks between gap candidates; drafts postcard-altitude restatement before room speaks) | Always present | Producer's standing job: catch and flag in real time |
| Time runs out before Move 5 completes | Medium | Compress Move 4 first, Move 1 second. Never compress Move 2 or Move 3. Move 5 is non-negotiable — its output is Slide 1 itself |

## 8. Trade-offs resolved

These trade-offs were considered during spec authoring and resolved as documented. Recorded here so the rationale survives future revision pressure.

| Trade-off | Resolution | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Sequencing: gaps before Center stress-test vs Center before gaps | **Gaps first, Center stress-test second** | v3.1 doctrine treats current-state Center as interpretive synthesis from cross-element evidence. Gap evidence is part of Center's evidence base; suppressing it before stress-testing Center would invert the diagnostic logic |
| Identity-vs-aspiration screen: standalone Move 4 vs embedded inside Move 5 | **Standalone Move 4 (8 min, discrete output)** | Embedded would bury the screen inside compile pressure where bias toward closure tends to silently re-import aspirational items |
| Slide 1 geometry: canonical Strategyzer triangle-three-circles vs Brightworks-aligned rectangularized | **Brightworks-aligned rectangularized, end-to-end (canvas + slide)** | Brightworks layout is a faithful adaptation of canonical Strategyzer SGF (Center anchors three petals, gaps live between adjacent petals); rectangular form gains scan-ability without losing tool fidelity; canvas-to-slide handoff becomes lock-and-postcard rather than spatial translation |
| Center anchoring arrows: binary green/red vs three-state green/yellow/red | **Three-state (green/yellow/red)** | Binary forces premature commitment — every yellow case rounds to false confidence or unwarranted escalation. Yellow arrows accumulate downstream debt that P1.2 clears in Decision 1 work, which is the right place for that debt to live |
| Move 1 element validation: flag-forward vs reopen-on-trigger | **Flag-forward (no element rework in Step 9)** | Reopening locked element content violates the U-shape and creates rework cascades. The escalation lever (cancel session at 48 h readiness check) is upstream, not in Move 1 |
| AI synthesis panel: left column vs floating frame | **Left column, full canvas height** | Continuous reference enforces ongoing interrogation discipline through all moves; floating frame would encourage the room to stop interrogating after Move 0 |
| Dissent panel structure: sectioned by move vs single chronological log | **Sectioned by source move** | Cleaner downstream traceability into the structured handoff doc; small loss of real-time texture is acceptable cost |
| Move 4 threshold protocol: halt-and-rerun (Option A) vs carry-forward (Option B) | **Option B with >70% threshold** | Preserves Slide 1 ship date and P1.2 sequencing; aspirational items become P1.2 Decision 1 input rather than session-stoppers; >70% failure remains the catastrophic-Step-8 trigger that does halt the session |
