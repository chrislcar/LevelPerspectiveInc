🎯 Key Practice: One Dominant Stage

For every customer item (Job, Pain, or Gain), assign ONE dominant stage only. This forces clear ownership, simplifies roadmapping, maintains clean metrics, and reduces cognitive overload.

Choose the appropriate framework based on what the customer item addresses:

🏥 Use CDVC

When: The item relates to the care journey—patient experiences, clinical touchpoints, or care coordination across the continuum.

Example: Scheduling appointments, receiving test results, post-treatment follow-up.

⚙️ Use Porter’s VC

When: The item involves managing the business of delivering care—internal operations, systems, processes, or organizational enablement.

Example: Staff training, EHR integration, supply chain management, billing operations.

📋 Standard Structure for Every Item

Type: Functional / Social / Emotional
Name: ≤ 6 words
Statement: “I want to … so I can …” (≤ 15 words)
Value-Chain Stage: Choose framework (CDVC or Porter VC), then pick ONE dominant stage
